Any live maximum security products/services/experiences should eventually include functionality to handle message/request/interaction delays and failures, and also, should probably implement clever dummy message/request/interaction features... Amongst many other things of course!

In terms of the debate surrounding homomorphic alignment and heteromorphic alignment in the FINA encryption regime, I have made an executive decision, to stick with the more 'Feng Shui' approach of homomorphic alignment, rather than pushing for the 'Brutalist' heteromorphic alignment. The reason is as follows... Proviso: We are going to always be enabling an optional chunking feature... and with this proviso in mind, along with the maybe not so obvious facet of networking and data packets, which tends to penalise even only slightly large data packet sizes (If you send large data packets, you are vulnerable to a failure-to-reach-destination (FTRD) outcome, which basically wastes unnecessarily large amounts of compute and time, compared to small data packets, where an FTRD would only waste nugatory amounts of compute and time), it seems wise to provide large data encryption sizes using a combination of simple homomorphic alignment and chunking... Also, given that implementing a heteromorphic alignment encryption regime would not solve the data size problem, but rather simply "kick the can down the road a little", I have very little appetite to provide this, even though in some usecases, it may infact be superior to homomorphic alignment approaches (For example file encryption and similar such things as this)!... Just so that you know, I could be wrong, but I currently do not believe that there is a cybersecurity differential between homomorphic and heteromorphic regimes. The only possible advantages that I can see are efficiency and storage size improvements, with the heteromorphic approach...
